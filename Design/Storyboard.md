# BloodInk — Storyboard: "Red Seasons"

> **Genre:** Dark Fantasy Stealth Action RPG
> **Tone:** Grim, morally grey, mournful. Violence is purposeful, never glorified.
> **Themes:** Extinction, complicity, cycles of violence, identity through body modification

---

## PART I: THE WORLD

### The Edict
A living curse woven into the bloodlines of six noble families a century ago. It targets orc biology at a fundamental level — thinning blood, weakening bones, reducing fertility. Each generation born since the Edict is weaker than the last. Orc elders estimate two generations remain before total extinction.

The Edict is not ambient. It is *sustained* by six Edictbearers — one per kingdom — whose blood acts as an anchor. If all six die, the curse unravels. If even one survives, the Edict persists at reduced strength.

### The Orcs — What Remains
- Population has collapsed from millions to roughly 12,000.
- Survivors live in scattered camps hidden in the Ashwild, a volcanic no-man's-land between kingdoms.
- Culture is fractured. Many traditions lost. Young orcs don't know what tusks are supposed to look like.
- The clans still exist in name: **Hollow Hand** (assassins), **Irontusk** (warriors), **Ashroot** (shamans), **Deepcradle** (caregivers/healers), **Boneknit** (crafters).
- Inter-clan tension simmers. The Irontusk faction wants open war. Ashroot wants spiritual retreat. Deepcradle wants negotiation with humans.

### Blood-Ink & Tattoos
- The Edict lives in the Edictbearers' blood. When harvested and distilled by a Needlewise (tattoo shaman), it becomes **blood-ink**.
- Tattooing blood-ink onto orc skin turns the curse against itself — controlled infection that grants power.
- Each tattoo is painful, dangerous, and permanent. There are physical and psychological side effects.
- Vetch is slowly becoming something new — neither fully orc nor human. A living counter-curse.
- The tattoos whisper. Fragments of the Edictbearers' memories bleed through.

## PART II: CHARACTERS

### Vetch — The Player Character
- **Role:** Last of the Hollow Hand assassin order
- **Age:** Mid-30s (old for a post-Edict orc — most don't live past 40)
- **Appearance:** Lean, scarred. Tusks are cracked and short (Edict symptom). Moves with practiced silence.
- **Personality:** Quiet, observant, dry humor that masks grief. Not cruel — kills with purpose, not pleasure.
- **Motivation:** Save the orc race. Underneath: prove that the Hollow Hand's way — precision over brute force — was always the right path.
- **Arc:** Begins as a weapon pointed at a target. Must decide what the orcs *should* survive as.

### The Needlewise (Mother Ashgrove) — SECRET IDENTITY
- **Known as:** The Needlewise, keeper of the old ink-craft
- **Presented as:** An ancient orc woman, half-blind, who speaks in riddles. The camp's most respected elder.
- **True identity:** The former High Shaman who *created* the Edict and gave it to the humans
- **Motivation:** She watched centuries of inter-clan orc warfare. She decided extinction was kinder than the cycle. Now she's not sure. She inks Vetch because she wants to see if an orc can carry the Edict without becoming a monster — if they can, maybe the species deserves to live.
- **Foreshadowing:** She knows too much about Edictbearer bloodlines. She never seems surprised by setbacks. The tattoos she gives are suspiciously well-designed for something she claims to be "rediscovering."
- **Reveal:** Act 5 or 6. The player should suspect before the confirmation.

### Rukh — The Handler
- **Role:** Vetch's contact, intelligence gatherer, voice-in-ear during missions
- **Background:** Former Irontusk warrior, crippled by the Edict (legs gave out). Now runs the network of orc spies embedded in human cities.
- **Personality:** Bitter, tactical, darkly funny. Cares about Vetch but would sacrifice them if the mission required it.
- **Arc:** Starts pragmatic. As the truth about the Needlewise emerges, must confront that the war he lost was *arranged*.

### Deepcradle Senna — Camp Healer
- **Role:** Monitors Vetch's physical condition as the tattoos accumulate
- **Personality:** Warm but clinical. Doesn't sugarcoat the toll the ink is taking.
- **Function:** Delivers health/tattoo system tutorials in-narrative. Warns when Vetch is changing.
- **Arc:** Becomes increasingly alarmed. Represents the "what are we becoming?" question.

### Elder Grael — Irontusk Leader
- **Role:** Wants open war, opposes the assassination approach
- **Personality:** Proud, loud, traditional. Not stupid — just impatient and dying.
- **Function:** Political pressure at camp. Represents the "fight them head-on" philosophy.
- **Arc:** Either becomes an ally (if Vetch proves results) or a threat (if missions take too long).

## PART III: THE KINGDOMS & TARGETS

Each kingdom is a self-contained stealth sandbox. 6-8 targets per kingdom, with the Edictbearer as the final/primary target and the rest being secondary kills that weaken defenses, unlock intel, or yield blood-ink.

---

### KINGDOM 1: THE GREENHOLD
**Theme:** Pastoral tyranny — beauty built on buried horror
**Motif:** Wheat, gold, sunlight, scarecrows
**Aesthetic:** Rolling farmland, whitewashed villages, golden cathedrals. Looks like paradise.
**Reality:** Built on former orc grasslands. Uses captured orcs as field labor. The "scarecrows" in the fields are orc skeletons mounted as warnings.

**Edictbearer:** **Lord Harlan Cowl** — A jovial, beloved governor who genuinely believes orcs are subhuman. Not a cackling villain — a man who sleeps soundly because he's never questioned the hierarchy.

**Targets (6-8):**
1. **Lord Harlan Cowl** (Edictbearer) — The governor. Resides in Goldmanor, the estate at the kingdom's center.
2. **Reeve Maren** — Overseer of the orc labor camps. Knows the locations of all imprisoned orcs.
3. **Sister Blessing** — Head of the local church. Preaches that the Edict is divine mercy. Has a relic that strengthens the local curse.
4. **Captain Thorne** — Commander of the Greenguard militia. Veteran orc-hunter.
5. **The Assessor** — Traveling tax collector who also "assesses" orc captives for sale.
6. **Silas Rootwarden** — A farmer who runs an underground orc-fighting ring in his barn.
7. *(Optional)* **Dame Cowl** — Harlan's wife. Runs the estate's intelligence network. Killing her weakens Harlan's security but is morally complex — she shelters escaped orc children in secret.

**Key Level Locations:**
- The golden wheat fields (open stealth, scarecrow disguises)
- The labor camps (infiltration/liberation opportunity)
- Goldmanor estate (classic mansion infiltration)
- The harvest festival (crowded social stealth)

**Intel/Lore Unlocked:** First hints that the Edict had an orc collaborator. Documents reference "the Shaman's Bargain."

---

### KINGDOM 2: THE DRENCH
**Theme:** Exploitation of the desperate
**Motif:** Water, pearls, chains, drowning
**Aesthetic:** Coastal city built on stilts and pontoons. Perpetual rain. Bioluminescent harbors. Beautiful and rotting.
**Reality:** Economy runs on deep-sea pearl diving using orc slaves. Orcs are "ideal" because the Edict makes them expendable — they die after a few dives anyway.

**Edictbearer:** **Admiral Voss Keelan** — A calculating naval commander who views orcs as materiel. Keeps the Edict's power concentrated in a pearl he wears as a pendant — an anchor-focus.

**Targets (7):**
1. **Admiral Voss Keelan** (Edictbearer) — Commands from his flagship, *The Undrowned*.
2. **Divemaster Grell** — Manages the orc diving operations. A sadist who enjoys the drownings.
3. **Pearl Merchant Opaline** — The kingdom's wealthiest trader. Funds Keelan's fleet in exchange for monopoly.
4. **Surgeon Hask** — "Modifies" orcs for deeper dives (gills cut into necks, lungs punctured for pressure). Claims it's medicine.
5. **Harbormaster Lenn** — Controls all movement in and out of the city. Corrupt, bribable, but dangerous.
6. **The Tide Priestess** — Spiritual leader who blesses the slave ships. Genuinely believes she's saving orc souls through labor.
7. **Wrack** — An orc turncoat who manages other orc slaves for human masters. Survival at any cost.

**Key Level Locations:**
- The harbor and docks (vertical stealth on rigging and stilts)
- Underwater slave pens (limited visibility, pressure mechanics)
- The flagship *Undrowned* (ship infiltration)
- The Pearl Market (social stealth, crowds, rain)
- Flooded lower city (stealth through waist-deep water)

**Intel/Lore Unlocked:** The Edict has a specific alchemical formula. Someone with deep shamanic knowledge designed it — not human magic.

---

### KINGDOM 3: THE FANE OF FLENSING
**Theme:** Cruelty disguised as knowledge
**Motif:** Bone, glass, lenses, dissection
**Aesthetic:** Mountain fortress carved into a glacier. Clean, white, clinical. Libraries and laboratories.
**Reality:** The kingdom's scholars vivisect orcs to study the Edict's biological effects. They call it research. Their medical advances — sold to all kingdoms — are built on orc suffering.

**Edictbearer:** **Provost Ilian Myre** — Head of the Fane. A brilliant, soft-spoken academic who has personally dissected hundreds of orcs and feels nothing because she's classified them as specimens.

**Targets (7):**
1. **Provost Ilian Myre** (Edictbearer) — Resides in the Inner Fane, behind layers of security and locked research wings.
2. **Dr. Carrow** — Lead vivisectionist. Keeps detailed journals (useful intel but horrifying to read).
3. **Lensmaster Obel** — Head of surveillance. The Fane is watched by crystal-lens cameras everywhere.
4. **Warden Stoss** — Runs the "specimen holding" facility where live orcs are kept.
5. **Courier Yael** — Transports research findings (and orc tissue samples) between kingdoms. Mobile target.
6. **Apprentice Davan** — Young, idealistic researcher who is starting to question the ethics. Can be turned as an informant OR killed. Player choice.
7. **The Archivist** — Maintains the Fane's historical records. Has documents proving the Edict's orc origin — heavily redacted.

**Key Level Locations:**
- The glacier approach (exposed, patrolled, cold mechanics)
- The outer library (scholar disguises, social stealth)
- The laboratories (horror — cages, specimens, failed experiments)
- The Inner Fane (high security, puzzle-locks)
- The Archive vault (contains the critical lore revelation)

**Intel/Lore Unlocked:** Full confirmation that the Edict was designed by an orc shaman. The formula matches Ashroot shamanic tradition. The Needlewise's clan.

---

### KINGDOM 4: THE VERDANCY
**Theme:** Predation as tradition
**Motif:** Antlers, hunting horns, green leather, trophies
**Aesthetic:** Vast old-growth forest. Treehouses and lodges. Hunting culture permeates everything.
**Reality:** "Conservation hunts" of orcs are an annual tradition. Nobles pay fortunes for guided orc hunts in "preserves" — fenced forests stocked with captured orcs.

**Edictbearer:** **Huntmaster General Brielle Ashford** — A legendary tracker. Hunts orcs not out of hate but because she considers them "the most dangerous game." Respects Vetch as prey — and that makes her more dangerous.

**Targets (8):**
1. **Huntmaster General Brielle Ashford** (Edictbearer) — Mobile. Hunts in the deep forest. You must hunt the hunter.
2. **Kennel Lord Mosswick** — Breeds the tracking hounds used to find orcs.
3. **Trophy Master Sevren** — Maintains the Great Lodge's trophy hall. Orc skulls on every wall.
4. **Ranger Captain Fenn** — Patrols the forest borders. Elite archer.
5. **The Gamekeeper** — Manages the orc "preserves." Keeps them alive just long enough for the next hunt.
6. **Lady Ashford the Younger** — Brielle's daughter. Being groomed as successor. Participates in hunts reluctantly.
7. **Bark** — A half-feral orc who has survived in the preserves for years by helping hunters find other orcs. Tragic figure.
8. **The Horner** — Ceremonial Herald who blows the horn to start each hunt. Killing him disrupts the ritual calendar.

**Key Level Locations:**
- The deep forest (tracking, traps, counter-hunting)
- The Great Lodge (classic infiltration, trophy hall)
- The orc preserves (liberation opportunity, stealth through dense woods)
- The Annual Hunt (timed mission — prevent/disrupt the hunt while it's active)
- Ashford's personal hunting ground (final confrontation, she's tracking you too)

**Intel/Lore Unlocked:** Brielle's family were the original human recipients of the Edict. Her ancestor met with an orc shaman willingly. A physical description of the shaman matches the Needlewise.

---

### KINGDOM 5: THE CRUCIBLE OF THE NAMED
**Theme:** Righteous genocide
**Motif:** Fire, scripture, judgment, white robes stained with ash
**Aesthetic:** A holy city carved into a dormant volcano. Cathedrals of basalt. Eternal flames. Chanting.
**Reality:** The theological center that declared the Edict a divine judgment. They didn't create it, but they *sanctified* it — gave the other kingdoms moral permission.

**Edictbearer:** **His Holiness, the Ashen Father Morvain** — Supreme pontiff. Elderly, frail, and utterly convinced that orc extinction is God's will. His faith is sincere, which makes him terrifying.

**Targets (7):**
1. **The Ashen Father Morvain** (Edictbearer) — Deep within the Crucible's inner sanctum. Heavily guarded by zealots.
2. **Grand Inquisitor Vael** — Hunts orc sympathizers and heretics among humans. Efficiently brutal.
3. **Sister Ember** — Runs the orphanage (for human children) funded by seizure of orc property. Beloved publicly.
4. **Cantor Lhess** — Composes the hymns and scripture that justify the Edict. Propaganda arm.
5. **The Branded** — A group of human penitents with orc-style brands who infiltrate orc camps as spies. Their leader is the target.
6. **Reliquary Keeper Orvane** — Guards the Crucible's holy relics, several of which are orc artifacts repurposed for human worship.
7. **Flame Warden Tace** — Controls the volcano's thermal vents used as execution chambers ("purification").

**Key Level Locations:**
- The pilgrim road (disguise among pilgrims, social stealth)
- The lower cathedral (public space, crowds, guards disguised as monks)
- The catacombs (beneath the city — orc bones, ancient pre-Edict history)
- The inner sanctum (volcanic heat, narrow passages, zealot guards)
- The Reliquary (orc artifacts that react to Vetch's tattoos)

**Intel/Lore Unlocked:** The original Edict pact document. Two signatures — one human (the first Edictbearer), one orc. The orc signature is in shamanic script. It reads: "For the children who will never have to fight."

---

### KINGDOM 6: THE ACCORD SPIRE
**Theme:** The machinery of complicity
**Motif:** Scales, treaties, bridges, neutral gray
**Aesthetic:** A massive tower-city built on a bridge spanning a canyon between two kingdoms. Neutral ground. Diplomatic. Bureaucratic.
**Reality:** The administrative heart that keeps the six-kingdom alliance functioning. Every atrocity is logged, filed, and stamped. The banality of evil.

**Edictbearer:** **The Accord itself.** There is no single bearer — the sixth anchor is distributed across the **Council of Scales**, six diplomats (one from each kingdom) who collectively sustain it. All six must die.

**Targets (8):**
1-6. **The Council of Scales** (six diplomats, each a mini-Edictbearer) — They never meet in one place. Must be picked off individually.
7. **Chronicler Vast** — Keeper of all treaties, including the Edict pact. Has the original orc signature.
8. **The Gray Warden** — Commander of the Spire's neutral peacekeeping force. Genuinely believes in the Accord's mission of stability.

**Key Level Locations:**
- The bridge-city market (dense social stealth)
- The treaty halls (disguise, eavesdropping, document theft)
- Private embassy suites (six distinct infiltrations)
- The Chronicler's vault (final lore revelation)
- The Spire summit (final confrontation space)

**Intel/Lore Unlocked:** Full truth. The Needlewise IS Mother Ashgrove. The contingency in the tattoos is revealed.

## PART IV: NARRATIVE ARC BY ACT

### ACT 1 — THE FIRST MARK (Kingdom 1: The Greenhold)
- Vetch is deployed on the first mission. Establish stealth gameplay, blood-ink loop.
- Tone: Controlled. Professional. "This is what we trained for."
- Camp scenes: Meet Rukh, Senna, Grael, the Needlewise. Establish relationships.
- First tattoos. First whispers from the ink.
- End: Harlan Cowl dies. The Edict weakens slightly. Orcs in camp feel it — a child is born with stronger tusks.

### ACT 2 — THE DROWNED AND THE DROWNING (Kingdom 2: The Drench)
- Vetch confronts the scale of orc suffering (slave divers).
- Moral complexity: The orc turncoat Wrack. Do you kill a fellow orc who chose survival?
- Tattoos are accumulating. Senna notes physical changes in Vetch.
- Rukh's intelligence network reveals inconsistencies in the Edict's origin story.
- End: Keelan dies. Vetch begins hearing full sentences from the tattoo-whispers — memories not their own.

### ACT 3 — UNDER THE LENS (Kingdom 3: The Fane of Flensing)
- Horror act. The laboratories. What has been done to orcs in the name of science.
- The Apprentice Davan choice: mercy or efficiency?
- The Archive reveals the Edict was designed by an orc. The camp reels.
- Grael accuses the Ashroot clan. Internal orc politics boil.
- The Needlewise deflects expertly. "Many shamans knew such things."
- End: Myre dies. Vetch's tattoos now visibly move on their skin. Something is wrong. Or something is *working*.

### ACT 4 — THE HUNTER AND THE HUNTED (Kingdom 4: The Verdancy)
- Inversion: Vetch must hunt a hunter who is hunting them back.
- Most gameplay-intensive kingdom. Cat-and-mouse in the forest.
- Brielle's family records physically describe the Needlewise. Vetch confronts her.
- The Needlewise admits she knew the original Edictbearers. Claims she "tried to stop them." Lies.
- Camp tension peaks. Grael demands the Needlewise be imprisoned. Rukh is unsure.
- End: Brielle dies with respect — she names Vetch "worthy prey." The tattoos pulse in response.

### ACT 5 — ASH AND SCRIPTURE (Kingdom 5: The Crucible of the Named)
- The theological heart. Vetch confronts the *justification* for orc genocide.
- The catacombs contain pre-Edict orc history — what they were before.
- The Edict pact document is found. The orc signature. "For the children who will never have to fight."
- Vetch recognizes the handwriting. The Needlewise's hand.
- **THE REVEAL:** Confrontation at camp. Mother Ashgrove admits everything.
  - She wrote the Edict. She gave it freely.
  - The orc wars were worse than what the humans did. Millions of orcs killing orcs.
  - She chose slow extinction over eternal slaughter.
  - The tattoos carry her contingency: if any orc absorbs all six Edictbearers' blood, she can activate a failsafe that makes the Edict permanent and painless — a gentle fadeout rather than the current slow torture.
  - She inks Vetch because she genuinely doesn't know the right answer anymore. She wants Vetch to choose.
- End: The camp fractures. Grael, Rukh, and Senna each have different reactions. Vetch must decide who to trust going into the final act.

### ACT 6 — THE ACCORD (Kingdom 6: The Accord Spire)
- The final kingdom. All threads converge.
- The six Council members must be killed in a single extended operation — leaving and returning resets security.
- Vetch is at full power. The tattoos are alive, moving, whispering, *wanting*.
- The Needlewise is either imprisoned at camp, has fled, or is present (depending on Act 5 choices).
- Rukh serves as handler for the last time.

## PART V: ENDINGS

### ENDING A — "Break the Edict"
Vetch destroys all blood-ink anchors. The Edict shatters. Orcs begin healing immediately. But the Needlewise was right about one thing — the old clan rivalries surface within days. Grael marshals the Irontusk for war. The cycle begins again. The orcs will survive, but *as what?*
- **Tone:** Bittersweet. Freedom with no guarantees.
- **Final image:** Vetch watches from a ridge as orc war-banners are raised. They look away.

### ENDING B — "The Gentle Dark"
Vetch activates the Needlewise's contingency. The Edict becomes painless — orcs will fade over a generation, but without suffering. No more cracked tusks, no more stillbirths. Just... fewer orcs, then none. Peaceful extinction.
- **Tone:** Mournful. Arguably the most compassionate choice — and the most horrifying.
- **Final image:** Vetch sits with the last orc children as they play. The tattoos are still. For the first time, nothing hurts.

### ENDING C — "The Counter-Edict"
Vetch turns the accumulated blood-ink outward. The curse reverses — directed at the six kingdoms. Humans begin to weaken, thin, die. Orcs recover. The weapon changes hands. The oppressed become the oppressors.
- **Tone:** Dark. Triumphant and sickening in equal measure. Vetch has become the thing they were sent to destroy.
- **Final image:** Vetch stands in the Accord Spire, tattoos blazing, as human cities burn on the horizon. The Needlewise's voice: "I made the same face, once."

## PART VI: CAMP / BASE SYSTEMS

### Camp Structure
- **The Ink Tent** — Where the Needlewise applies tattoos (skill tree / upgrade screen)
- **Rukh's Map Table** — Mission briefing, target selection, intel review
- **Senna's Shelter** — Health check, tattoo side-effect management, lore dialogue
- **The Fire Circle** — Talk to NPCs, hear clan stories, political updates
- **Grael's Post** — Irontusk tension meter, optional side objectives
- **The Rookery** — Spy reports, world-state updates between missions

### Between-Mission Flow
1. Return from kingdom
2. Blood-ink harvest scene (narrative beat)
3. Talk to camp NPCs (new dialogue based on targets killed)
4. Visit the Ink Tent (spend ink on tattoos)
5. Senna checks your condition (health/tattoo UI)
6. Rukh briefs next mission
7. Deploy

## PART VII: TATTOO SYSTEM (GAMEPLAY)

### Tattoo Categories
| Category | Body Location | Effect Type |
|----------|--------------|-------------|
| **Shadow Marks** | Arms | Stealth abilities (invisibility, silent movement, shadow dash) |
| **Fang Lines** | Chest/Torso | Combat power (damage, speed, counter-attacks) |
| **Vein Scripts** | Legs | Movement (sprint, climb, water breathing) |
| **Skull Wards** | Face/Head | Perception (enemy detection, trap sense, dark vision) |
| **Spine Chains** | Back | Resistance (damage reduction, poison immunity, curse resistance) |
| **Whisper Rings** | Hands/Fingers | Interaction (lockpicking, pickpocket, disguise enhancement) |

### Ink Economy
- **Primary targets** (Edictbearers) yield **Major Ink** — unlocks new tattoo *categories*
- **Secondary targets** yield **Lesser Ink** — fills out tattoos within existing categories
- **Optional targets** yield **Trace Ink** — minor upgrades, cosmetic tattoo variations
- Tattoo side effects increase with total ink absorbed (visual changes, whispers, narrative consequences)

## PART VIII: STEALTH SYSTEMS (DESIGN NOTES)

### Detection Model
- **Visual** — Light/shadow, line of sight, disguises
- **Audio** — Footstep noise, environmental sounds, distractions
- **Scent** — Dogs and certain enemies can smell orc blood. Masking scent is a mechanic.
- **Arcane** — Some targets have ward-stones that detect blood-ink. Must be disabled.

### Stealth Tools
- **Ash Bombs** — Obscure vision and scent
- **Bone Chimes** — Audio distraction
- **Blood Lure** — Orc blood bait to redirect tracking animals
- **Shadow Step** — Tattoo ability, short-range teleport between shadows
- **Corpse Hide** — Play dead among orc remains (kingdom-specific)
- **Disguise Kit** — Human clothing, posture mimicry (limited duration, fails near dogs)

### Alert Levels (per kingdom)
1. **Unaware** — Normal patrols
2. **Suspicious** — Increased patrols, locked doors
3. **Alerted** — Active search, reinforcements
4. **Hunted** — Kingdom-wide lockdown, Edictbearer goes to safe room
5. **Siege** — You've been fully identified. Escape or fight through.
